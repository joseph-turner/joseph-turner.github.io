Actually, my first name is Brian, but that’s not important. What is important is that I'm a Front End Developer and I love what I do. I've spent the past {{time_from_beginning}} strengthening my skills. My design background offers me great insight into the vision of a design and an attention to detail that can be hard to find in the average developer.

What should I call you?

I've always thought websites were too unidirectional. But then again that's kind of the point. A website's user usually comes to a site seeking to receive some sort of information. In you're case you're probably here to learn more about me and my skills abilities and inteterests. (That's my hope at least.) The problem with this is that I'm terrible at talking about myself. Well, that's sort of a lie now isn't it. All humans almost always like talking about their inteterests with somebody. I just don't like talking about my "accomplishments" or my "skills." It's always made me feel like I'm bragging about myself and I'm not particularly fond of people that brag of their own acheivements. I do enjoy, however, talking about the things that I enjoy.

My path to becoming a front end developer wasn't exactly a straight one. I grew up with strong desires to go into photography. I always imagined working for National Geographic, travelling to distant lands and capturing foreign landscapes and wild animals. Using my dad's Canon A-1, I took pictures of everything and, eventually, gained an eye for composure.

But, alas, my interest shifted moderately to physical therapy to psychology. I learned a lot of interesting things about human psyche and what makes people think and act the way they do. But psychology wasn't for me either.

I was in my 3rd year of my Bachelor's program when I realized I didn't want to deal with other people's mental health issues. I don't think I would have handled that well. So my first instinct was to go back to my original plan of photography. So I changed my major to art and that's where I met Graphic Design.

My eyes were opened with the design world. I learned to apply my sense of composure in new ways. I learned about the wonderful world of typography, that changed everything for me. I learned about pace and rhythm, scale and contrast, negative space, focal points, and all the other elements that go into design.

I noticed that I was fairly good at critiquing and collaborating, but only fair at creating designs of my own. Probably because I was much more technically minded that many of the other students. In a pioneer web design class I discovered that I excelled at HTML and CSS and tutored many of my classmates.

My first job out of school was a Web Design position at Scentsy which morphed over time into Front End Development. And here I stay, playing to my strengths with an eye for detail and an understanding of the design and it's intention, an understanding of the user and their perspective, and an understanding of HTML and CSS that is hard to match. I think I've found my calling.
